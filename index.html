About Me

I am Abraham Chan Deng garang, a first-year Software Engineering student at the African Leadership University in Kigali. My passion is to empower the youth by providing them with opportunities for growth and employment through my planned project, Educare. Through Educare, I aim to connect young people with essential skills and opportunities to prepare them for a brighter future. My goal is to utilize my knowledge and skills to bridge the gap between job seekers and employers, ensuring that every young individual has access to meaningful employment. I believe that technology can serve as a powerful tool for positive change in our communities, and I am committed to mentoring and developing the next generation. In addition to my studies, I actively engage in community outreach programs, inspiring and uplifting others. Ultimately, I strive to ensure that every young individual has access to the resources they need to succeed.

Projects

I am working on a project called Educare , which aims to connect youth with available job opportunities that match their skill sets. Through this initiative, I plan to apply my studies in software engineering to develop an application and website that will bridge the gap between job seekers and employers. This platform will enable young individuals to showcase their skills, view job opportunities, and directly interact with potential employers, breaking down barriers that limit hiring rates in our country.

In addition to job matching, I intend to establish training programs that equip youth with essential skills. Many teenagers face challenges due to a lack of financial resources and emotional support to pursue their dreams. Therefore, I aim to empower them through workshops in various fields, including IT, business management, and vocational skill development, enabling them to become self-sufficient.

This effort will not only assist in immediate employment but will also foster a sense of entrepreneurship and innovation among the youth.
